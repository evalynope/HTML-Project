J.R.R. Tolkien: The Greatest Of All Time

The scholar and author who shaped modern fantasy through his timeless tales of courage, language, and myth.

Who is J.R.R. Tolkien?

J.R.R. Tolkien was an English writer and professor, best known for authoring The Hobbit, The Lord of the Rings trilogy, and other related works which became a monumental foundation to fantasy literature. He was a fellow of three colleges at Oxford, a member of the informal literary group 'The Inklings', and a close colleague of the scholar and author C.S. Lewis. His works draw on themes such as friendship, folklore, and the struggle between good and evil. Many regard his as the 'Father of modern fantasy.'

What makes Tolkien the GOAT?

  • Tolkien was a soldier in World War I, and his wartime experiences shaped the depth and realism of his writing.
  • He modeled the elf Lúthien after his wife, Edith, and had “Beren and Lúthien” inscribed on their shared headstone.
  • His close friendship with C.S. Lewis played a major role in Lewis’s conversion to Christianity.
  • Tolkien was a linguistic genius, creating over a dozen fictional languages and mastering 13 real-world languages.
  • Tolkien is widely regarded as the “father of modern fantasy,” influencing countless writers and filmmakers.
  • Tolkien was a talented artist and painted beautiful illustrations of many locations and maps mentioned in his books.
